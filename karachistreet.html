Karachi Street Food

Karachi’s street food mirrors the heart of the city—fast-paced, diverse, and bursting with flavor. From the sizzling Waheed Fry Kabab of Burns Road to rainbow Gola Ganda by the Clifton beach, every bite carries a memory. Vendors pass down their secret spice blends and recipes through generations, turning ordinary streets into flavor-packed landmarks. In Karachi, street food isn’t just eaten—it’s experienced, celebrated, and deeply woven into everyday life.

Burns Road Bun Kabab

History: Born on Burns Road, Bun Kebabs are Karachi’s spicy answer to burgers. Made with shami patties, chutney, and fried eggs in a soft bun. Once a working-class meal, now a beloved street food for all. You’ll find them sizzling late into the night across the city.

Ingredients: Shami kabab, egg, bun, onions, green chutney, ketchup.

Recipe: Toast bun, fry kabab and egg, assemble with chutney and onions. Serve hot and spicy with chutney.

Waheed Fry Kabab

History: Waheed’s kababs are fried in butter and masala at high heat, known for their sizzling sound and rich aroma.

Ingredients: Beef kabab mince, butter, green chilies, spices.

Recipe: Mix kabab meat with spices, fry in butter till brown and crisp. Serve with naan and onions.

Student Biryani

History: Started as a humble stall in Saddar, Karachi, feeding local students. Its flavorful biryani quickly won hearts across the city. Today, it's a legendary brand known far beyond Pakistan. The perfect mix of spiced rice, tender meat, and tradition.

Ingredients: Chicken/beef, rice, potatoes, yogurt, fried onions, biryani masala.

Recipe: Marinate meat, cook biryani masala, layer with rice and potatoes, steam cook ("dum") until aromatic.

Fish Katakat

History: A Karachi-style twist on the classic katakat, packed with prawns, squid, and fish. Popular around Kemari and beachside stalls, especially in the evenings. Vendors skillfully chop and fry seafood on a sizzling tawa. It’s loud, fast, buttery, and full of spicy coastal flavor. .

Ingredients: Boneless fish, butter, tomatoes, green chilies, spices.

Recipe: Cook fish on a hot tawa with spices using katakat-style chopping, serve sizzling with naan.
