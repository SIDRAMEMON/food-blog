Explore KpK Cuisine

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK) province in Pakistan has a diverse economy, with agriculture and forestry playing significant roles, and is known for its unique cuisine. The province contributes significantly to Pakistan's GDP and mining output, and its food culture includes dishes like chapli kebab and kabuli pulao.

Chapli Kebab

History: Chapli kebab, a flat, minced meat patty, originates from the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK) region of Pakistan, formerly known as NWFP. The name "Chapli" likely comes from the Pashto word "chaprikh" or "chapleet," meaning flat or patty-like, referring to its shape. The dish is believed to have been created in Peshawar and is a popular street food in both Pakistan and Afghanistan. .

Ingredients:KPK (Khyber Pakhtunkhwa) Chapli Kabab, a popular Pakistani dish, is made with minced meat (beef, lamb, or mutton), onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, green chilies, and a blend of aromatic spices like coriander seeds, cumin seeds, pomegranate seeds, and chili powder. Other key ingredients include cornflour or gram flour, eggs, and herbs like coriander and mint.

Recipe: Mix minced meat with chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, crushed coriander, and pomegranate seeds. Add egg, cornflour, red chili, salt, and spices to bind the mixture. Flatten the mixture into round kababs and shallow fry them on medium heat. Serve hot with naan, chutney, and salad

Kabuli Pulao

History: Kabuli Pulao, a flavorful rice dish, has its roots in Afghanistan, specifically Kabul, and is also a cherished dish in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK) province of Pakistan. It's known for its sweet and savory taste, featuring rice cooked with meat, carrots, raisins, and nuts. The dish's history is intertwined with the cultural exchange between Afghanistan and Pakistan, particularly among Pashtun communities

Ingredients: Kabuli Pulao, a fragrant and flavorful rice dish, is made with basmati rice, meat (usually lamb or mutton), carrots, raisins, and a blend of aromatic spices. Key ingredients also include onions, ginger-garlic paste, oil, and various spices like cumin, cardamom, and cinnamon. The dish is known for its sweet and savory taste, with the sweetness coming from the caramelized carrots and raisins, and the savory from the meat and spices.

Recipe: The meat is cooked with whole spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves until tender. Separately, rice is boiled with salt and then layered with the meat mixture. Caramelized carrots, raisins, and almonds are sautéed in oil and added on top. Everything is steamed together (dum) for perfect blending of sweet and savory flavors.

Dumpukht

History: Dum Pukht, a slow-cooking method originating in Central Asia and Persia, is deeply rooted in the culinary traditions of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK), Pakistan, particularly in Peshawar. It's known for its rich, aromatic flavors achieved by slow-cooking meat and vegetables in a sealed pot, often in a clay pot, over a low flame or buried in hot coals.

Ingredients: KPK Dumpukht, a slow-cooked Peshawari dish, typically includes mutton (or lamb), goat fat, potatoes, tomatoes, onions, green chilies, ginger, garlic, salt, pepper, and water. Lemon juice and a dough seal (made from flour and water) are also essential for the cooking process.

Recipe: Dumpukht is a slow-cooked meat dish sealed and cooked on low flame. Meat is marinated with yogurt, onions, garlic, ginger, and whole spices. It is placed in a pot and sealed with dough to trap the aroma. The dish is cooked on very low heat for several hours until tender. It is served hot with naan or rice, rich in smoky and spiced flavor.

Shinwari Karahi

History: Shinwari Karahi, a popular Pashtun dish, originates from the Shinwari tribe, known for their hospitality and love of food, in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK) province of Pakistan and Afghanistan. The dish is believed to have originated in the Shinwari market area of KPK.

Ingredients: The core ingredients include meat (lamb, mutton, or chicken), tomatoes, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, black pepper, salt, and oil or ghee. Optionally, fresh coriander and julienned ginger are used for garnishing.

Recipe: Take fresh lamb or chicken and cook it in oil with salt and green chilies. Add chopped tomatoes and let them soften without using any extra spices. Cook on high flame until the meat releases its natural flavor and becomes tender. Mash the tomatoes into the meat to form a light gravy. Serve hot with naan for an authentic Shinwari taste.
