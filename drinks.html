Traditional Pakistani Drinks

Cool your tastebuds with refreshing beverages straight from the streets and homes of Pakistan.

Lassi

History: Lassi originated from Punjab and is a centuries-old drink traditionally made by villagers to beat the summer heat.

Ingredients: Yogurt, water, sugar or salt, cardamom (optional), ice cubes.

Recipe: Blend yogurt and water in equal portions. Add sugar for sweet lassi or salt for salty lassi. Optionally, add crushed cardamom and ice, blend until frothy and serve chilled.

Sattu Drink

History: Sattu is a traditional drink from rural areas of Punjab and Sindh, known for its cooling and energizing properties in harsh summers.

Ingredients: Sattu flour (roasted gram powder), cold water, sugar or jaggery, lemon juice, black salt.

Recipe: In a glass of cold water, mix 2 tablespoons of sattu flour, sugar or jaggery to taste, lemon juice, and a pinch of black salt. Stir thoroughly and serve immediately.

Imli Aaloo Bukhara Drink

History: Popular in street stalls of Karachi and Lahore during Ramzan, this tangy drink is both refreshing and digestive.

Ingredients: Tamarind (imli), dried plums (aaloo bukhara), sugar, black salt, cumin, water.

Recipe: Soak tamarind and plums in water for 4–5 hours. Strain and mash the pulp, then cook it with sugar, cumin, and black salt. Chill and serve cold.

Mint Lemonade (Podina Nimbu Pani)

History: A common household drink across Pakistan, especially in summers, known for its cooling and digestive properties.

Ingredients: Fresh mint leaves, lemon juice, water, sugar, black salt.

Recipe: Blend mint leaves, lemon juice, sugar, and water. Strain the juice, add black salt, stir well, and serve chilled over ice.
