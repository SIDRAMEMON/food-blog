Explore Street Food of Pakistan

A food street is a special place where many small food stalls, shops, and restaurants are found on the same road. These streets are popular for offering tasty local food at affordable prices. The first food street in Pakistan was made in Gawalmandi, Lahore. After that, many more were built, such as Melody Food Street and Blue Area Food Street in Islamabad, Burns Road in Karachi, Ghanta Ghar Food Street and Qissa Khwani Bazaar Food Street in Peshawar, and Anarkali Food Street in Lahore. In 2012, a new and famous food street opened near the Badshahi Mosque and Lahore Fort. It is called Fort Road Food Street. Pakistani street food is loved for its bold flavors, wide variety, and low cost.

Gol Gappay

Crunchy puris filled with tangy tamarind water, spicy potatoes, meethi chutney and chickpeas. A favorite snack at every street corner.

Bun Kabab

A spicy kabab, usually a shami kabab, is placed inside a toasted bun along with chutney, onions, and sometimes a fried egg, making it Pakistan’s own style of street burger.

Chana Chaat

A refreshing mix of boiled chickpeas, onions, green chilies, tomatoes, and spices. Served cold with lemon juice and chutneys.

Gola Ganda

Shaved or crushed ice, soaked in vibrant flavored syrups and sometimes topped with milk, makes for a perfect summer treat especially popular among kids and teenagers.

Samosa

Deep fried triangular snack filled with spicy potatoes or minced meat or vegetables. Served with chutney.

Pakoray

Deep-fried fritters made from gram flour batter mixed with onions, potatoes, or spinach. Crispy and spicy, usually served with chutney or ketchup.

Masala Fries

Thick cut flavourfull potato fries sprinkled with red chili and chaat masala . Served hot with ketchup or spicy mayo on the streets.
