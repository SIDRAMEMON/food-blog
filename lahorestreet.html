Lahore Street Food

Lahore’s street food reflects the soul of the city—vibrant, bold, and unapologetically flavorful. From the rich, buttery Butt Karahi to the tangy Gol Gappay of Liberty Market, every bite tells a story. Streets like Gawalmandi, Anarkali, and Fort Road Food Street overflow with sizzling pans, aromatic spices, and late-night laughter. Here, food isn’t just a meal—it’s culture, community, and centuries of Mughal-inspired taste shared on every plate.

Butt Karahi

History: A Lahori icon, Butt Karahi is known for its rich tomato base and sizzling wok service. Cooked fresh in front of you, it's a flavor-packed experience. People flock from across Pakistan to try it at the original shop. Spicy, buttery, and full of tender chicken or mutton chunks.

Ingredients: Chicken/mutton, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, butter, green chilies.

Recipe: Cook meat in butter with crushed tomatoes and spices. Garnish with green chilies and ginger before serving hot in a karahi.

Lahori Chargha

History: This spiced whole chicken is deep-fried to perfection and loved at food streets. Originating in Lahore, it became a popular centerpiece for parties and feasts. Known for its vibrant red color and crispy skin. Chargha is bold, spicy, and proudly Lahori.

Ingredients: Whole chicken, yogurt, lemon juice, red chili, garam masala, vinegar.

Recipe: Marinate chicken overnight in spices and yogurt. Steam or boil slightly, then deep-fry until golden and crisp.

Gawalmandi Samosay

History: These crunchy triangle snacks are a Gawalmandi staple. Stuffed with spicy potatoes or mince, they're fried golden and sold hot. Locals pair them with sweet and tangy chutney. Perfect for evening chai or a quick street bite.

Ingredients: Flour dough, potatoes/mince, spices, green chili, coriander.

Recipe: Fill dough with spicy mixture, fold into triangle, and deep fry until crispy. Serve with tamarind or mint chutney.

Murgh Cholay

History: A rich combo of chicken and chickpeas served hot with naan. You’ll find it steaming on tavas early in the morning. A true Lahori breakfast classic with bold spices. It’s hearty, spicy, and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients: Chicken, chickpeas, onions, tomatoes, spices, green chilies.

Recipe: Sauté onions, add tomatoes and spices, cook chicken and chickpeas together until tender. Garnish and serve hot with naan.
