Eid Special Dishes

Eid is a celebration of gratitude, love, and delicious feasts shared with family and friends. Pakistani households prepare iconic dishes that reflect joy and tradition. From rich meaty curries to delightful desserts, every bite holds a connection to home and culture. Here are some traditional Eid dishes that bring families together around the table.

Sheer Khurma

History: A luxurious Eid dessert made with milk and vermicelli, popular in Muslim households across Pakistan.

Ingredients: Milk, vermicelli, dates, sugar, ghee, cardamom, dry fruits.

Recipe: Heat ghee and lightly fry vermicelli. Add milk and cook until thick. Add chopped dates, sugar, cardamom, and dry fruits. Simmer for 10–15 minutes and serve warm or chilled.

Beef Biryani

History: A royal rice dish, often prepared on Eid-ul-Adha using marinated beef or mutton.

Ingredients: Beef, basmati rice, yogurt, onions, tomatoes, spices, saffron.

Recipe: Marinate beef in yogurt and spices. Cook with fried onions and tomatoes until tender. Layer with half-cooked rice and saffron water. Steam (dum) on low heat and serve hot with raita.

Qorma

History: A rich Eid curry made with aromatic spices, served with naan or sheermal.

Ingredients: Chicken or beef, onions, yogurt, ginger garlic paste, whole spices, ghee.

Recipe: Fry onions until golden, then blend to make a paste. Add meat and spices, yogurt, and cook till oil separates. Add blended onions and simmer till meat is fully tender and masala thickens.

Shami Kabab

History: A traditional minced meat patty served during Eid brunch or tea-time.

Ingredients: Beef mince, chana daal, ginger, garlic, cloves, cinnamon, eggs.

Recipe: Boil mince with spices and lentils. Grind the mixture and form round patties. Dip in egg and shallow fry until golden brown. Serve with mint chutney.
