Peshawar Street Food

Peshawar’s street food is a living story of heritage, flavor, and tradition. As you walk through the lively alleys of Qissa Khwani Bazaar or Namak Mandi, the smoky aroma of sizzling Chapli Kababs fills the air, inviting you closer. Steaming pots of Kabli Pulao bubble gently beside piles of spices and dry fruits, while tea stalls offer hot Kehwa with a side of conversation. Every dish here isn’t just a meal—it’s a memory passed from generation to generation. In Peshawar, food is a celebration of culture, a symbol of hospitality, and a proud reflection of Pashtun identity.

Chapli Kabab

History: The most iconic food of Peshawar, Chapli Kabab gets its name from its slipper shape. Cooked over open flames, it's a symbol of local pride.

Ingredients: Beef mince, onions, tomatoes, crushed coriander, pomegranate seeds, chilies.

Recipe: Mix all ingredients, shape into flat patties, fry in oil until crispy. Serve with naan and chutney.

Namak Mandi Karahi

History: Found in the famous Namak Mandi food street, this karahi is made with only 3–4 ingredients but explodes with flavor.

Ingredients: Lamb, tomatoes, green chilies, salt, and fat (dumba tail or ghee).

Recipe: Fry meat in fat, add tomatoes and chilies, cook until oil separates. Serve sizzling hot with naan.

Dumba Karahi

History: Made from the fatty meat of dumba (fat-tailed sheep), this karahi is rich, buttery, and found in traditional roadside dhabas.

Ingredients: Dumba meat, tail fat, garlic, salt, tomatoes.

Recipe: Cook meat in its own fat, add garlic and tomatoes. Let it simmer till tender and aromatic.

Kehwa (Green Tea)

History: A traditional green tea served in Peshawari hujras and after every heavy meal. It's a symbol of hospitality and culture.

Ingredients: Green tea leaves, cardamom, sugar or honey (optional).

Recipe: Boil water with cardamom, add tea leaves, simmer and strain. Serve in small cups with dates or sweets.
